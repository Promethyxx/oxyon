# Manifesto

## I. Knowledge as a Common Good
Knowledge must remain public, free, and accessible.
Today, it is hidden behind (pay)walls and proprietary silos.

## II. Arsenal Pragmatism
Consistency lies in the project's direction, not in the tools used.
Proprietary software can and will be used to build Free and Open Source Software (FOSS).
Efficiency of action takes precedence over infrastructure purity.

## III. Resilience
The multiplication and decentralization of sources is the only way.
Data must be mirrored, archived, and distributed to survive censorship and platform decay.

## IV. Build
Promote and contribute to FOSS.
The goal is to automate the liberation, organization, and preservation of data.
Every line of code must make the user more autonomous and knowledge harder to gatekeep.
Everytime you need a proprietary tool, think to build a free one!
